## About

I am a PhD candidate in Immunology at the University of Colorado Anschutz Medical Campus, where I study immune regulation in chronic lung diseases, including COPD and pulmonary fibrosis.

My research integrates macrophage biology, mucosal immunology, and glycobiology to uncover mechanisms with therapeutic relevance. I am interested in understanding immune system-mediated consequences in the lung and their implications for disease pathogenesis and therapy. Previously, I trained at Harvard Medical School and Colorado State University, developing a strong foundation in respiratory biology.

---

## Research Interests

## Technical Expertise

### Experimental Immunology and Lung Biology
Immunoassays including flow cytometry (FACS), ELISA, RT‑qPCR, and immunohistochemistry.  
Extensive experience with murine models of lung disease, including bronchoalveolar lavage and multiple routes of administration (intratracheal, intranasal, intravenous, intraperitoneal, subcutaneous, and oral gavage).  
Blood collection (submandibular and cardiac), anesthesia, and euthanasia procedures.

### In Vitro and Tissue Models
In vitro airway systems including air–liquid interface cultures and 3D airway/lung organoids.  
Bacterial culture and infection‑relevant experimental workflows.

### Computational Biology and Data Analysis
Analysis of high‑throughput sequencing data including bulk and single‑cell RNA‑seq and ATAC‑seq.  
Experience working in high‑performance computing environments using Linux and R.

### Software and Instrumentation
Microsoft Office, GraphPad Prism, FlowJo, ImageJ, Zeiss ZEN Blue, FlexiVent, Visiopharm, and QuPath.
